# Product Vision

## Goal

Help everyday homeowners imagine how their yard could look and feel before they commit time, money, or labor.

## Product thesis

- Most homeowners know a yard feels unfinished, awkward, or high-maintenance, but struggle to translate that into a concrete plan.
- A single uploaded yard photo can anchor more helpful landscaping guidance than a generic article or Pinterest board.
- Consumers want visual reassurance before buying plants, materials, or outdoor furniture.
- The winning experience blends aspiration with practical next steps: concept imagery, plant or material direction, and phased guidance.

## Core user promise

Upload a photo of your yard, explain the outcome you want, choose how many concepts to generate, and receive visual landscaping directions plus a practical outdoor brief you can actually use.

The experience should feel premium, hopeful, and easy to trust on both desktop and mobile, with especially low friction for phone-based capture.

New visitors should be able to create one yard project and receive one complimentary concept before they are asked to sign in and buy credits.

## Primary audience

- Homeowners improving front yards, backyards, patios, garden beds, and curb appeal
- Consumers who want outdoor inspiration but still need realistic constraints like budget, maintenance, and climate awareness
- People who are comfortable paying for a better result if the app helps them avoid costly trial and error

## Monetization

- One complimentary guest concept before sign-in and paid usage are required
- One-time credit packs purchased through Polar
- Social login with Google and Apple before paid generations, saved history, and billing
- Each paid job charges a configurable base analysis cost plus added credits per requested concept image
- Pricing is derived from model costs and a configurable target margin so operators can tune the business over time
- Future affiliate revenue from plant, lighting, material, or furniture recommendations

## Success criteria

- Users understand the app on the first landing-page visit
- First-time users complete the upload-to-result flow without assistance
- Generated concepts feel plausible enough to share or save
- Recommendations feel specific enough to guide a real project or shopping list
- The app increases confidence, not just inspiration

## Non-goals for MVP

- Professional landscaper team workflows
- Exact measurement-grade landscape architecture output
- AR placement or real-time 3D walkthroughs
- Native mobile apps
- HOA compliance review automation
